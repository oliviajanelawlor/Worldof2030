How we got here


NASA reports that scientists have attributed the global warming trend to the human expansion of the "greenhouse effect" - warming that results when the atmosphere traps heat radiating from Earth towards space.

Certain gases in the atmosphere block heat from escaping. Long-lived gases that remain semi-permanently in the atmosphere do not respond physically or chemically to changes in temperature. These are described as "forcing" climate change.

Gases that contribute to the greenhouse effect include:

Water Vapour | Nitrous Oxide | Methane | Carbon Dioxide

On earth, human activities are changing the natural greenhouse. Over the last century, the burning of fossil fuels like coal and oil has increased the concentration of atmospheric carbon dioxide. This happens because the coal/oil burning process combines carbon with oxygen in the air to make CO2. To a lesser extent, the clearing of land for agriculture, industry and other human activities has increased the amount of greenhouse gases.

We have already explored the impact of climate change and global warming, with warmer conditions, melting glaciers, increasing sea levels.
Our impact
The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change concluded that there is a more than 95% probability that human activities over the past 50 years have warmed our planet.

The industrial activities that many of our every day-to-day tasks depend on has raised Carbon Dioxide levels from 280 parts per million to 400 parts per million in the last 150 years.

Since 1850, almost all the long-term warming can be explained by green house gas emissions and other human activities, Carbon Brief Analysis finds.

The Paris Climate Agreement (Paris Agreement Under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change is an international treaty which aims to reduce the emission of gases that contribute to global warming. Named after the country it was adopted in, went into force on November 4th 2016 and has since been signed by 197 countries. By November 2019, 187 of those countries had ratified to the agreement.
